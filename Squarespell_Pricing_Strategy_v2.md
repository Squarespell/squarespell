## 1. AUDIT FINDINGS

### The Competitive Landscape (What I Researched)

I looked at every quiz funnel tool your users might compare you to:

| Tool | Cheapest Paid Plan | What You Get | Leads Included |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Interact** | $39/mo ($29/mo annual) | 500 leads, limited features | 500/mo |
| **ScoreApp** | $39/mo ($29/mo annual) | 3 scorecards, 100 responses/mo | 100/mo |
| **involve.me** | $29/mo annual | 2 funnels, basic features | Low |
| **Outgrow** | $22/mo ($14/mo annual) | 5 content pieces, 250 leads/mo | ~250/mo |
| **Typeform** | $29/mo ($25/mo annual) | 100 responses/mo | 100/mo |
| **Opinion Stage** | $25/mo | Pro features | Limited |
| **Squarespell (current)** | $12/mo ($9/mo annual) | 3 quizzes, 500 leads/mo | 500/mo |

**Your current pricing is already a massive competitive advantage.** At $9/mo annual, you're cheaper than every single competitor — and you offer MORE leads than most of them. Interact charges $29/mo annual for the same 500 leads you give at $9/mo. ScoreApp charges $29/mo annual for just 100 responses.

Raising prices would destroy this advantage. Your instinct is correct.

### Why Your Current Structure Feels Broken (Despite Good Prices)

**Problem 1: Starter ($12/mo) is a trap, not a plan.**
Starter gives users 3 quizzes and 500 leads — that's fine. But it strips branching logic, integrations, email sequences, A/B testing, and scheduling. Without branching logic, a user can't build a real quiz funnel. They can only build a flat multiple-choice quiz. So Starter users never see real results, never hit their lead limit, and either churn or leap straight to Pro. Starter isn't generating upgrades — it's generating cancellations.

**Problem 2: The feature cliff between Starter and Pro is too steep.**
Starter has 3 features turned on. Pro has 11. That's not a tier difference — it's a different product. There's no gradual value ladder. It's "basic quiz maker" at $12 and "full marketing platform" at $25. Nothing in between.

**Problem 3: Business only adds 3 features over Pro.**
Pro gives you unlimited quizzes, 2K leads, A/B testing, branching, all integrations, email sequences, advanced analytics, custom CSS, scheduling, and AI generation. Business adds white-label, custom domain, and team seats. For $24/mo more. Unless you're an agency, there's no pull toward Business.

**Problem 4: No lead add-ons exist.**
You sell email add-on packs ($5/$15/$29) but there are zero lead add-ons. When a user hits 500 leads on Starter, their quiz silently stops collecting. The user doesn't know why conversions died. There's no impulse-buy option — just "upgrade your whole plan." You're losing both revenue and trust.

**Problem 5: Email limits aren't enforced.**
The audit found email quotas are tracked but never enforced at send time. Users on Starter can send unlimited emails despite a "500/mo" display in the dashboard. The email add-on packs you sell supplement a limit that doesn't actually exist.

**Problem 6: Trial-to-Starter cliff is brutal.**
Trial gives Pro-level features for 14 days. If a user picks Starter, they lose branching logic, integrations, A/B testing, email sequences, scheduling — everything they used to build their first funnel. Their quiz literally breaks. This doesn't encourage upgrading to Pro; it encourages leaving.

**Problem 7: "Responses" vs "Leads" inconsistency.**
The pricing page says "responses" in some places. The dashboard says "leads." Plans.ts says "leads." The billing page says "leads." Users don't know if these are the same thing.

### What Squarespace Does Right (and How to Adapt It)

Squarespace's pricing works because of four principles:

**1. Every tier is usable.** Their Basic plan ($16/mo) lets you build a complete website with SSL, templates, and basic SEO. It's not crippled. You can succeed on Basic — you just can't sell online. That's the natural upgrade trigger: "I need ecommerce."

**2. Each tier adds one clear category of value.** Basic → Core adds ecommerce. Core → Plus adds advanced ecommerce (shipping labels, no transaction fees). Plus → Advanced adds enterprise features (APIs, abandoned cart). You always know what you're paying more for.

**3. The middle tier is the obvious choice.** Core is positioned as "Most Popular" with visual highlighting. It has the best price-to-value ratio. The majority of users land there.

**4. Annual discount is framed in dollars, not percentages.** "Save $120/year" hits harder than "Save 28%." They default to showing annual pricing.

### Where Revenue Is Being Lost

1. **Starter churn:** Users can't build real funnels → leave within 30 days
2. **Zero lead add-on revenue:** No impulse-buy when limits hit
3. **Phantom email add-ons:** Selling packs for unenforced limits
4. **No conversion triggers:** Only prompt is a dismissible trial banner
5. **Weak Business pull:** Pro users have no reason to upgrade
6. **Annual framing:** Not leading with annual pricing or dollar savings

## 2. NEW PRICING TABLE (ZERO PRICE INCREASES)

### Plan Names: Core / Pro / Business

I'm keeping **Pro** and **Business** because they already have brand recognition and Stripe subscriptions. Renaming "Starter" to "Core" because "Starter" sounds like a throwaway tier. "Core" sounds foundational — like something you'd actually want.

### Pricing: IDENTICAL to Current

| | Core | Pro ★ Most Popular | Business |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Monthly** | **$12/mo** | **$25/mo** | **$49/mo** |
| **Annual** | **$9/mo** ($108/yr) | **$19/mo** ($228/yr) | **$39/mo** ($468/yr) |
| **Annual Savings** | Save $36/yr (25%) | Save $72/yr (24%) | Save $120/yr (20%) |

**Zero changes.** Same prices. Same annual discounts. The revenue improvement comes entirely from repackaging, add-ons, and conversion optimization.

### Why These Prices Already Work

**Core at $9/mo annual** — Cheaper than every competitor's entry tier. Cheaper than a single Squarespace extension. This is your acquisition engine. The goal isn't revenue from Core — it's getting users in the door and building habits that drive upgrades.

**Pro at $19/mo annual** — Still cheaper than Interact ($29/mo), ScoreApp ($29/mo), involve.me ($29/mo), and Typeform ($25/mo) at their cheapest annual tiers. And you give MORE: unlimited quizzes, 2,000 leads vs. their 100-500. This is your profit center. 70% of paying users should be here.

**Business at $39/mo annual** — Agency tier with unlimited everything + white-label. Comparable to ScoreApp's Business at $74/mo. Your users get more for roughly half the price.

### The Squarespace User Math

A typical Squarespace user pays $23/mo (Core plan) for their website. Adding Squarespell:
- Core: $23 + $9 = $32/mo total → very reasonable for a website + quiz funnel
- Pro: $23 + $19 = $42/mo total → still less than most competitors' entry tier alone
- Business: $23 + $39 = $62/mo total → agency pricing, expected at this level

Nobody is going to blink at $9/mo for a quiz tool when they're already paying $23/mo for Squarespace. Even $19/mo feels proportional. That's the sweet spot.

Users who see results don't cancel — they upgrade because they want A/B testing to optimize, integrations to connect their CRM, and email sequences to nurture leads. The upgrade trigger becomes "I'm succeeding and want more" instead of "my quiz broke and I'm leaving."

ScoreApp gives branching logic (they call it "jump logic") on every plan, including free. Outgrow gives basic logic jumps on their $14/mo plan. Not having branching on your paid entry tier is leaving money on the table by killing users before they convert.

**Clear Upgrade Reason:** "I want A/B testing to optimize my quiz, or integrations to send leads to my CRM, or email sequences to nurture leads automatically."

### The Upgrade Math (How This Drives Revenue)

**Scenario A — Core user at 1,000 leads, wants more:**
They buy Lead Boost (+500 for $4/mo). Total: $16/mo for 1,500 leads.
→ Pro gives 3,000 leads for $25/mo. System shows: *"Get 2x the leads + A/B testing + integrations for just $9/mo more."*

**Scenario B — Core user buys two add-ons:**
Lead Boost ($4) + Email Boost ($5) = $21/mo total on Core.
→ Pro is $25/mo with 3x the leads, 3x the emails, and every feature unlocked.
→ System shows: *"You're spending $21/mo. Upgrade to Pro for $25/mo and unlock everything."*

**Scenario C — Pro user at 3,000 leads, running hot:**
They buy Lead Max (+3,000 for $15/mo). Total: $40/mo for 6,000 leads.
→ Business is $49/mo with unlimited everything.
→ System shows: *"Upgrade to Business for $9/mo more and never worry about limits again."*

**Scenario D — Pro user buys Lead Plus + Email Plus:**
$9 + $12 = $21/mo in add-ons. Total: $46/mo.
→ Business is $49/mo with unlimited everything.
→ System shows: *"You're spending $46/mo. Go unlimited for just $3/mo more."*

**This is the design principle:** Add-on packs are priced so that buying 2+ add-ons on any tier costs close to or more than upgrading. Users discover this organically, or the announcement bar tells them. Either way, they move up.

**Add-ons aren't the business model — they're the upgrade funnel.**

2. **Lead add-on infrastructure:** New `user_addons` table in Supabase tracking purchased packs. Modify `insert_lead_with_limit_check` RPC to check base_limit + addon_limit.

3. **Add-on billing:** Create Stripe products for each lead/email pack. Monthly recurring, cancellable from billing page.

## 5. ANNUAL vs MONTHLY STRATEGY

### Framing Rules (Inspired by Squarespace)

**Rule 1: Default to annual pricing.**
The pricing page toggle defaults to "Annual." Annual prices are the prominent numbers. Monthly is secondary. Just like Squarespace.

**Rule 2: Show dollar savings, not just percentages.**
"Save $72/year" hits harder than "Save 24%." Use dollar amounts on the toggle and on each plan card.

| Plan | Monthly | Annual | Toggle Badge |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core | $12/mo | $9/mo | "Save $36/year" |
| Pro | $25/mo | $19/mo | "Save $72/year" |
| Business | $49/mo | $39/mo | "Save $120/year" |

**Rule 3: Global toggle badge anchors the highest savings.**
Next to the Annual/Monthly toggle: **"Save up to $120/year"**

**Rule 4: Show the annual price as the "real" price.**
On each plan card, annual price is large and primary. Monthly price is shown smaller, struck-through or greyed, with "or $X/mo monthly" treatment.

Example for Pro card:
> **$19**/mo
> billed annually — save $72/year
> *$25/mo if billed monthly*

**Rule 5: No fake urgency.**
Don't do "limited time" or "special offer." Squarespace users are savvy. Just present annual as the smart choice. The savings speak for themselves.

## 6. IN-APP ANNOUNCEMENT BAR STRATEGY

### Priority System

Only one bar shows at a time. Highest-priority active bar wins.

| Priority | Trigger | Color | Dismissible? |
|---|---|---|---|
| P0 | Payment failed | Red | No |
| P1 | Trial expired | Red | No |
| P2 | Trial ≤ 3 days left | Amber | Once/day |
| P3 | Leads at 100% (quiz stopped) | Red | No |
| P4 | Emails at 100% (sequences paused) | Red | No |
| P5 | Add-on spend ≥ next tier price | Teal | Once/week |
| P6 | Leads at 80%+ | Amber | Once/week |
| P7 | Emails at 80%+ | Amber | Once/week |
| P8 | Trial active (>3 days) | Teal | Yes (permanent) |
| P9 | Feature promo / announcement | Teal | Yes (permanent) |

### Messaging for Each Variant

**P0 — Payment Failed** (Red bar, not dismissible)
> Your payment failed. Update your payment method to keep your quizzes live.
> **[Fix Billing →]**

**P1 — Trial Expired** (Red bar, not dismissible)
> Your free trial has ended. Pick a plan to keep capturing leads — plans start at $9/mo.
> **[View Plans →]**

**P2 — Trial Ending** (Amber bar, resets daily)
> {X} days left in your trial. Keep everything you've built — plans start at $9/mo.
> **[Choose a Plan →]**

**P3 — Lead Limit Hit** (Red bar, not dismissible)
> You've hit your {limit} lead limit. Your quizzes stopped collecting leads.
> **[Buy More Leads]** | **[Upgrade Plan]**

**P4 — Email Limit Hit** (Red bar, not dismissible)
> You've reached your {limit} email limit. Email sequences are paused.
> **[Buy More Emails]** | **[Upgrade Plan]**

**P5 — Add-On Upsell** (Teal bar, resets weekly)
> You're spending ${total}/mo on add-ons. Upgrade to {next_plan} for ${price}/mo and get more for less.
> **[See the Savings →]**

**P6 — Leads Approaching** (Amber bar, resets weekly)
> You've used {current} of {limit} leads this month ({percent}%).
> **[Add More Leads →]**

**P7 — Emails Approaching** (Amber bar, resets weekly)
> You've sent {current} of {limit} emails this month ({percent}%).
> **[Add More Emails →]**

**P8 — Trial Info** (Teal bar, permanent dismiss)
> Welcome! You have {X} days to explore everything. No credit card needed.
> **[Explore Features →]**

**P9 — Promo/Announcement** (Teal bar, permanent dismiss)
> Used for new feature announcements, tips, etc.

### Quick-Buy Flow (From Bar)

When a user clicks "Buy More Leads" or "Buy More Emails":

1. A slide-out panel opens (not a full page redirect)
2. Shows the 3 add-on options with prices
3. Below: a comparison — *"Or upgrade to {next_plan} for ${price}/mo and get {leads} leads included"*
4. User picks → Stripe checkout → instant activation
5. Bar updates or disappears

This keeps users in the dashboard. No friction. No page redirect for a $4 purchase.

### Behavior Rules

1. **One bar at a time.** Never stack. Highest priority wins.
2. **Sticky at top.** Below nav, above page content. Pushes content down.
3. **No animation.** No bouncing, flashing, or sliding. Urgency comes from color + copy.
4. **Non-dismissible bars** (P0-P1, P3-P4): Represent broken functionality. Stay until resolved.
5. **Timed dismiss** (P2, P5-P7): Come back on schedule (daily or weekly). Stored in localStorage with date key.
6. **Permanent dismiss** (P8-P9): Gone forever once dismissed.

## 8. STRATEGIC REASONING — WHY THIS WORKS

### 1. You become the cheapest real quiz funnel tool in the market.

At $9/mo annual with branching logic and 1,000 leads, Squarespell Core offers more than Interact at $29/mo (500 leads), ScoreApp at $29/mo (100 responses), or Typeform at $25/mo (100 responses). This is your marketing headline: *"Full quiz funnels from $9/mo. Other tools start at $29."*

### 2. Core users can actually succeed now.

Branching logic means Core users build real funnels. 1,000 leads means they see real results. Users who see results don't cancel — they grow into Pro. The upgrade trigger shifts from frustration ("my quiz broke") to ambition ("I want A/B testing to optimize").

### 3. Add-ons create a new revenue stream AND drive upgrades.

Lead add-ons (which don't exist today) catch every user who hits their limit. At $4-15/mo per pack, they generate immediate impulse revenue. But the pricing math ensures that buying 2+ add-ons costs nearly as much as upgrading to the next tier. Users either pay for add-ons (new revenue) or upgrade (more subscription revenue). You win either way.

### 4. The announcement bar catches every conversion moment.

Right now, conversion happens by accident — a user stumbles onto the pricing page. The new system actively catches: limit approaching (80%), limit hit (100%), add-on overspend vs. upgrade, trial milestones, and payment failures. Research shows that pricing page clarity can improve conversion 30-50%. In-app triggers on top of that compound the effect.

### 5. Email enforcement unlocks phantom revenue.

You've been selling email add-on packs for limits that aren't enforced. Once enforcement goes live, users who actually hit email limits will buy packs. This is free revenue from infrastructure you've already built.

### 6. No price increase means zero churn risk.

Every existing subscriber keeps their current price or gets MORE for the same price. Current Starter users get 2x leads, branching logic, and scheduling for the same $12/mo. Current Pro users get 50% more leads (2K→3K) for the same $25/mo. Nobody has any reason to downgrade or cancel.

### Revenue Impact (Conservative Estimate)

| Driver | Mechanism | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Lead add-on packs | New revenue stream (doesn't exist today) | +8-12% ARPU |
| Email enforcement | Real add-on purchases for enforced limits | +3-5% ARPU |
| Reduced Core churn | Better plan → users stay → upgrade later | -20% churn on entry tier |
| Announcement bar | Active conversion prompts at key moments | +10-15% upgrade rate |
| Add-on → upgrade pipeline | Math drives users to next tier | +5-8% plan upgrades |
| Annual framing | Dollar savings + default annual toggle | +5-10% annual adoption |

**Combined: 15-25% revenue increase with zero price changes.**
